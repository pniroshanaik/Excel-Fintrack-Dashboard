## 🔍 Overview

The Excel Fintrack Dashboard is an interactive financial analysis project built using Microsoft Excel. It focuses on analyzing sales, profit, and discount patterns across products, segments, countries, and time periods to support data-driven decision-making.

## 🎯 Business Problem

Organizations often struggle to identify:

* Which products and segments drive the most revenue and profit
* How sales vary across regions and time
* The impact of discounts on profitability
* Seasonal trends affecting performance

This dashboard addresses these challenges by providing clear and actionable insights.

## 🚀 Key Features

* 📊 KPI tracking for Sales, Profit, and Discounts
* 🎛 Interactive slicers for dynamic filtering
* 📈 Trend analysis across countries and time periods

## 🧹 Data Preparation

* Identified and handled missing values in key fields such as Sales, Profit, and Date components
* Created calculated columns for:

  * Gross Sales = Units Sold × Sale Price
  * Sales = Gross Sales − Discounts
  * Profit = Sales − COGS
* Derived Month and Year fields for time-based analysis

---

## 📈 Key Insights

### 1. Product & Segment Performance

* Paseo is the highest revenue and profit-generating product
* Government segment contributes the largest share of both revenue and profit
* Enterprise segment shows lower profitability due to margin pressure

🔑 **Key Takeaway:**
Government–Paseo combination is the most valuable revenue driver

---

### 2. Sales Trends (Time & Country)

* Sales peak significantly in October across countries
* Germany and France show the strongest performance
* Sales decline sharply in November and December

🔑 **Key Takeaway:**
Sales are highly seasonal and driven by specific peak periods

---

### 3. Discount Impact

* Medium discounts generate the highest revenue
* Low discounts yield higher profitability
* High discounts reduce overall profit

🔑 **Key Takeaway:**
Optimizing discount strategy is critical for balancing revenue and profit

---

### 4. Seasonality

* Strong seasonal trend observed with peak in October
* Decline in later months indicates event-driven demand

🔑 **Key Takeaway:**
Sales are not evenly distributed and depend on seasonal factors
